About Me

Ever since I was young, I've had a fascination with computers and troubleshooting problems, fueled by an insatiable curiosity for understanding how things work in the digital realm. My journey into the world of technology has not only involved mastering the art of diagnosing and resolving technical issues but also delving into the realm of coding languages. Recognizing coding as the backbone of modern computing, I've embraced the challenge of learning various programming languages to enhance my problem-solving capabilities. Whether it's deciphering the intricacies of Python, mastering the logic of Java, or exploring the versatility of HTML and CSS, I am committed to honing my coding skills to complement my troubleshooting expertise. My passion for learning coding languages stems from a desire to not only understand the inner workings of software but also to leverage this knowledge to innovate and create impactful solutions in the ever-evolving landscape of technology.


What Accessability Means To Me

Color blindness and visual impairments run in my family and I've grown up around amazing people who have worked making both the digital world and the real world more accessible. A core memory of mine is hearing from a cousin who worked as a accessibility consultant for local governments online accessibility and lamenting at how difficult many official webpage were to navigate with a screen reader; and the current trends at the time were to remove accessible features that were seen to be unsightly for the average user. As I'm now creating websites I think back to that moment constantly and push myself to bridge the gap between sleek modern presentation and accessibility with every line of code I write.
